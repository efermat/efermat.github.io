Historical review of research and development on linear antennas in Tohoku University

Authors: Kunio Sawaya

Source: FERMAT, Volume 24, Communication 2, Nov.-Dec., 2017

Abstract: Invention and development of the Yagi-Uda antenna in Tohoku University, Japan are described. Communication experiments in VHF and UHF frequency bands using transmitter and receiver developed in the same university as well as the Yagi-Uda antenna are also presented. Then, self-complementary antennas, which is the frequency independent antenna invented in Tohoku University are described. Analysis methods of large loop antennas is also presented.

Index Terms: Directive short-wave antenna, Yagi-Uda antenna, self-complementary antenna, constant impedance, log-periodic structure, large loop antenna, twin loop antenna, IEEE Milestone
